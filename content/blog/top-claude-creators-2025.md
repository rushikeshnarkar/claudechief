# 10 Claude Creators You Should Follow in 2025

The Claude ecosystem is exploding — and it's the creators who are driving it. Prompt engineers, workflow architects, automation specialists — the people building the systems that thousands of teams rely on every day.

Here's who you should be following right now. These creators are consistently shipping quality content, pushing the boundaries of what's possible with Claude, and building real communities around their work.

## How We Selected These Creators

We looked at:
- Quality and consistency of published skills/workflows
- Engagement and community response
- Originality of approach
- How actively they're sharing knowledge

These aren't necessarily the biggest accounts. They're the ones doing the best work.

## The Top 10

### 1. Anthropic Official

Start here. Anthropic's team publishes the most important Claude updates, research, and cookbook examples. Their official documentation is also the best reference for advanced usage.

**What they publish:** Official model updates, research papers, cookbook examples, and best practices.

### 2. The Prompt Engineer Collective

A community-driven collective that publishes high-quality prompt patterns for enterprise use cases. Their work focuses on making complex AI workflows accessible to non-technical teams.

**What they publish:** Structured prompt libraries, workflow templates, case studies from enterprise deployments.

### 3. AI Operations (AIOps) Practitioners

The people talking about how to actually run AI in production. Not just "here's a cool prompt" — but "here's how to monitor it, evaluate it, and improve it at scale."

**What they publish:** Evaluation frameworks, monitoring dashboards, production deployment guides.

### 4. The Automation Architects

These creators focus on the intersection of Claude and workflow automation. If you want to connect Claude to your entire software stack, these are the people to follow.

**What they publish:** MCP integrations, Zapier/Make workflows, API combinations, no-code automation.

### 5. Vertical AI Specialists

Creators who go deep on specific industries: legal, healthcare, finance, education. They build domain-specific skills that handle the nuance of regulated industries.

**What they publish:** Industry-specific workflows, compliance-aware prompts, sector case studies.

### 6. The Prompt Artists

For these creators, the craft is in the writing. They focus on making prompts that are elegant, readable, and endlessly reusable. Their prompts are works of art.

**What they publish:** Beautifully structured prompts, prompt writing tutorials, style guides.

### 7. The Educators

Creators who specialize in teaching AI literacy. They assume no prior knowledge and build learning paths for teams getting started with Claude.

**What they publish:** Tutorials, courses, learning roadmaps, beginner guides.

### 8. The Researchers

Creators who push the boundaries of what's possible. They experiment with novel prompting techniques, test new models, and publish their findings.

**What they publish:** Research posts, benchmark results, experimental techniques.

### 9. The Community Builders

These creators focus on building the community itself: organizing events, running Discord servers, facilitating prompt sharing, and connecting creators with each other.

**What they publish:** Community news, event recaps, creator spotlights.

### 10. The Indie Hackers

Solo builders using Claude to ship products faster. They're the ones turning AI capabilities into MVPs in days, not months.

**What they publish:** Product teardowns, build-in-public journeys, indie hacker workflows.
